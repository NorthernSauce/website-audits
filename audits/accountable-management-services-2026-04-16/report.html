The site describes what Accountable does clearly enough, but reads like an internal memo — it talks about the business rather than the client's situation, there's no social proof anywhere, and one of the homepage images is accidentally loading from another web designer's client server.
Top 5 priority fixes
1
Add testimonials — the site has zero social proof
The site claims to be "well established" with "many years of experience" but there is not a single testimonial, review, or client outcome anywhere. For a service asking someone to hand over their finances, this is the single biggest conversion barrier on the site.
Action: Collect 3–5 testimonials from existing clients. Each needs a name, business type, and a specific outcome: time saved, tax refund received, deadline crisis averted. Place at least one directly on the homepage, next to the contact CTA.
2
Fix the broken image loading from another client's server
Two images on the homepage are loading from woodenhousejoinery.osam-dev.co.uk — another business's development server. This is a live bug: if that server goes offline or the files move, the images on this site break entirely. It also raises questions about the site's technical maintenance.
Action: Replace both images immediately. Download them and re-upload to the Accountable WordPress media library, or replace with original images. Check all other image sources to confirm no others are externally hosted.
3
Rewrite the hero to open with the client's problem
"Puts you in financial control" is better than most accountants' heroes — it hints at an outcome. But the subtext immediately reverts to "Accountable Management Services Ltd is here to make your lives easier. We're the complete accounting firm..." — the business becomes the subject again within one sentence.
Action: Keep "Puts you in financial control" as the hook. Follow it with a sentence that names the specific frustration: "Sole traders and small businesses in the Midlands spend too much time on their books and not enough on the business. We fix that."
4
Remove or replace all vague credibility claims
"Speed and professionalism is at the heart of our high-quality work", "friendly and efficient service", "many years of experience" — these phrases appear on every accountant's website in the country. They're non-falsifiable and add zero trust. A visitor has no idea what "many years" means or what "high-quality" looks like in practice.
Action: Replace each vague claim with a specific one. "Many years of experience" → "Andrea has been running Accountable since 2006." "Friendly and efficient" → a testimonial that demonstrates it. Specifics convert; generalities don't.
5
Make Andrea the face of the business
The site is entirely anonymous — no photo of Andrea, no personal story, no indication of who the client is actually going to be working with. People hire accountants they trust. Trust comes from people, not from company descriptions. The "well established business" framing actively works against this.
Action: Add a section — or a dedicated About page — with Andrea's photo, her background (she's been doing this since 2006, that's nearly 20 years of experience), and a sentence about why she started the business. Real people build real trust.
