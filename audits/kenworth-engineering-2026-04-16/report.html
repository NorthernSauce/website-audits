The verdict
Your site looks professional and the technical content is thorough — but there's almost no social proof despite working with Formula 1 teams and global companies, the copy talks about capabilities rather than client outcomes, and the lack of any real person on the About page makes it hard for a prospect to build trust before they pick up the phone.
Top 5 Priority Fixes
1
Add client testimonials and show the companies you work with
Your site claims to work with "some of the world's largest companies" across automotive, F1, oil & gas, renewable energy and more — but there isn't a single client name, logo, quote, or outcome anywhere on the site. For a B2B precision engineering business where trust is everything, this is a significant gap. Prospects making big decisions about who machines their parts need proof before they pick up the phone.
Action: Gather 3–5 testimonials from existing clients with their name, company, and a specific outcome (delivery time hit, tolerance achieved, problem solved). Add at least one client logo strip to the homepage. Even a short case study would do more work than anything else on the site.
2
Put a real person on the About page
Your About page is a short paragraph about the company's history and capabilities. There's no mention of Natalie or the family behind the business, no photos of the team, and no personal story. For a family-run business with 30+ years of history, that's a missed opportunity — people buy from people, and "family-owned" is a differentiator that needs a face and a story to land.
Action: Rewrite the About page around the real story — who started it, why, what drives the business today, who the customer is talking to when they call. Add a photo of the team on the shop floor. Authentic beats professional here.
3
Rewrite the copy to lead with what clients get, not what you do
Every page on your site talks about Kenworth's capabilities, machines, and processes. The visitor already knows they need CNC parts. What they're trying to work out is whether you're the right supplier. Copy that explains what working with you means for the client — right first time, on time, no rejected batches, no delays — is far more persuasive than a list of capabilities.
Action: Rewrite the hero subheading and the opening of each service page to lead with the client outcome before explaining how you achieve it. The machine specs can stay — just move them further down the page.
4
Deal with the stale blog — update it or remove it
Your News & Insights section shows three posts, the most recent from April 2023. A blog that hasn't been touched in three years signals an inactive business to anyone who spots it, and it's likely doing more harm than good.
Action: Either remove News & Insights from the main navigation and archive the existing posts, or commit to publishing something quarterly. If you're not going to maintain it, removing it is the better option.
