The copy and positioning are genuinely impressive for a firm this size — but the complete absence of full-attribution testimonials, case studies, and a personal story means visitors have to take quality claims entirely on faith.
Top 5 priority fixes
1
Add real testimonials with full attribution
The site has testimonials but they're abbreviated — first name only, no role, no business, no specific outcome. "Sarah — so helpful!" carries almost no conversion weight. A named person, their situation, and a specific result they got is the difference between decoration and proof.
Action: Contact 5–10 clients and ask for a short written testimonial. Ask them specifically: what was the problem before working with you, and what changed? Use full name, business name, and ideally a headshot. Add these throughout the site near CTAs, not just in a dedicated reviews section.
2
Add a personal story — who are Gabriella and Anthony?
The site mentions Gabriella's FMAAT qualification and Anthony by name but gives visitors almost nothing to connect with. People buy from people. A brief, honest story about why they set up My Trusted Accountant and what they're trying to do differently would build more trust than any credential alone.
Action: Write a proper About page — 200–300 words — that tells the story of why the business exists, includes a real photo of Gabriella and Anthony, and connects their background directly to the client's problem (the stress and confusion of managing self-employed finances).
3
Replace mailto contact with a proper enquiry form
Clicking "Get in touch" opens the visitor's email app with a blank compose window. This creates friction (wrong app opens, have to type from scratch), loses context, and gives the business no record of the enquiry. Some visitors — particularly on mobile — simply won't bother.
Action: Replace the mailto link with an embedded contact form (Tally, Typeform, or similar free tools integrate easily into Next.js). Include a field for their name, email, what they need help with, and how they found you. Add a confirmation message explaining what happens next and when to expect a reply.
4
Add at least one detailed case study
With 500+ clients, there must be at least one story worth telling — a freelancer who was drowning in receipts, a sole trader who didn't realise they were owed a refund. A single detailed case study with a before/after structure is worth more than ten generic testimonials when it comes to convincing a sceptical visitor.
Action: Pick one client (with permission) and write a 300–400 word case study: their situation before, what you did, and the specific outcome. Publish it as a page and link to it from the homepage and services pages.
5
Reduce developer dependency for site updates
The site is built on Next.js and hosted on Vercel. While this gives excellent performance, adding a testimonial, updating pricing, or changing a service description likely requires a developer to push code changes. Sites that can't be updated easily go stale — and stale sites lose trust.
Action: Integrate a headless CMS (Sanity, Contentful, or Notion as a data source) for the content that will change regularly — testimonials, services, FAQs, team bios. This keeps the Next.js performance while giving non-technical editors a proper interface to keep content fresh.

The site is built on Next.js, a React framework that requires code changes and a deployment pipeline to update content.
