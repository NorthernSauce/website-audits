overall
Your site lists what you do but gives visitors almost no reason to choose you — there are no testimonials, no personal story, no outcome-focused copy, and no easy way to get in touch without picking up the phone.
Top 5 priority fixes
1
Add testimonials — you have zero social proof right now
Your site asks visitors to trust you with their finances, but there's nothing on it from anyone who's done exactly that. No reviews, no quotes, no names. In a market where every accountant claims to be "dedicated and enthusiastic," social proof is often the only thing that tips someone into getting in touch.
Action: Ask three or four of your happiest clients for a short quote — just two or three sentences about what it's like to work with you and what's different. Add their name, their business type, and ideally a year. Put them on the homepage where visitors can see them without scrolling to the bottom.
2
Add a contact form — email links aren't enough
Right now, getting in touch with you means copying an email address and opening a separate email client. That's friction. A lot of people who are interested will just move on rather than bother. A simple embedded form — name, email, brief message — removes that barrier entirely.
Action: Your site runs on WordPress with the Divi builder — adding a contact form takes about ten minutes using a free plugin like WPForms or Contact Form 7. Put it prominently on the homepage (not just at the bottom) and make sure submissions come straight to your inbox.
3
Add a clear call-to-action above the fold
A visitor landing on your homepage has to scroll past all your content to find any contact details at the bottom. If they don't scroll — and many won't — they have no obvious next step. Every page should have one clear button near the top that tells visitors what to do next.
Action: Add a "Get in touch" or "Book a free call" button in your hero section, right below your headline. Make it a proper button — not just a text link — so it stands out. This one change alone will increase the number of people who get in touch.
4
Rewrite the copy to lead with your client's problem, not your services
"A modern approach to accounting" followed by a list of services (accounts preparation, bookkeeping, VAT returns) describes what you do, not what your clients get from working with you. A visitor can find this same list on any accountant's website. The question they're really asking is: "Will working with you make my life easier?"
Action: Start with the problem. Something like: "Running a small business is relentless. Your accounts shouldn't be another thing on your list." Then explain what working with you actually means for them — not the services, but the outcome. What does their life look like after they hire you?
5
Add a personal about section — show who Jules is
Jules' LinkedIn headline says "Refreshingly different & definitely more than just the numbers!" — that's a real personality and a genuine differentiator. None of that comes through on the website. People hire accountants they like and trust, and right now your site gives them no reason to feel either.
Action: Add a short about section with a real photo of Jules, two or three sentences about your background, and — most importantly — why you do things differently. The "more than just the numbers" angle is a strong hook. Use it on the site, not just on LinkedIn.
