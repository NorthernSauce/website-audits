The site confirms Jason is a qualified accountant but gives no compelling reason to choose him — the copy talks entirely about the business, there's no visible social proof, and the GoDaddy template undersells what is clearly a properly licensed, regulated professional.
Top 5 priority fixes
1
Rewrite the homepage copy to speak to the client's problem
The current hero says "Accounting You Can Count On" — a tagline about the service, not the client. Small business owners are stressed about HMRC deadlines, confused by tax, and want to focus on their business not their books. The copy should open there.
Action: Replace the hero headline and subtext with copy that names a specific frustration (e.g. "Spending more time on your books than your business?") and positions the service as the relief — not just a list of what it covers.
2
Add testimonials — specific, outcome-based, attributed
The "Reviews" section in the nav appears to be empty. There is zero social proof anywhere on the site. For a service business asking someone to hand over their finances, this is a serious trust gap. "Great to work with" testimonials are worthless — outcomes and specifics convert.
Action: Collect 3–5 testimonials from clients. Each should name the outcome: time saved, tax saved, stress removed. Add photos where possible. Place at least one directly next to the "Book Today" CTA.
3
Define a specific niche and lead with it
The service list spans bookkeeping, payroll, VAT, five types of tax, audit, forensic accounting, and company secretarial. That's a generalist offering competing on price. Jason has AAT licensing and real credentials — the site should say who his ideal clients are and why he's the right fit for them specifically.
Action: Identify the 2–3 client types Jason most enjoys working with and gets the best results for. Build the homepage messaging around those clients. The right visitors will self-identify; the wrong ones will self-select out.
4
Replace the GoDaddy template with a professional site
Jason is an AAT-licensed, regulated accountant. The GoDaddy Website Builder template looks like a 2020 SME starter site — the design actively contradicts the level of professional credibility the footer credentials are trying to establish.
Action: Rebuild on a platform that reflects the quality of the service. The visual design is a first impression before any copy is read — it needs to match what Jason is actually charging and delivering.
5
Update the copyright year and sort the blog
The footer shows © 2023. It's 2026. This is a small signal that carries disproportionate weight — a visitor who spots it wonders what else hasn't been maintained. The blog section also needs to be either actively published or removed.
Action: Update the copyright year to 2026. Check when the last blog post was published — if it's more than 6 months ago, either commit to a publishing schedule or remove the blog from the nav entirely.
